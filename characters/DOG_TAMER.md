# THE DOG TAMER
## Character Production Sheet

---

## Overview

**Role:** First Antagonist (Episodes 1-4)
**Classification:** Demon general. Malgris's tracker and first dispatch.
**Function in story:** He is the catalyst. His arrival kills the mother. His presence forces the powers to activate. His defeat sends the signal to Malgris that changes the scale of the story.

The Dog Tamer does not speak. He does not need to. His presence is a statement. His defeat at the hands of two untrained teenagers who have had their power for less than a week tells Malgris more than any report could.

---

## Physical Description

**Build:** Tall. Wrong proportions. Too tall -- 6'8" or more, and it is not broad or strong-seeming, it is stretched. The joints are slightly misaligned. The head tilts naturally, as if the neck does not hold it at the correct angle.
**Skin:** Grey-brown. Wrong texture at proximity -- like skin that has not moved correctly for a long time.
**Face:** Obscured. The face is always in shadow -- hood up, head tilted, lighting arranged so the detail never lands cleanly. In profile, the structure is recognisably humanoid. Directly facing, the detail stays wrong, unclear.
**Eyes:** Not visible. The eye area is always dark. If they exist, we do not see them in Season 1.
**Hair:** If it exists, it is not visible. The hood is always up.

**Movement:** This is the most important visual note. The Dog Tamer is still more than he moves. When he stands at the far end of a walkway, he does not shift weight, does not turn his head, does not acknowledge the world around him in any normal way. He simply is in a location.

When he moves, it is deliberate and slow. He does not chase. He walks toward. The idea that walking toward someone slowly is more threatening than running has to be earned visually -- the sound design and the stillness do this.

---

## Clothing

**Long dark coat:** Floor length. Very dark -- almost black, but with a very slight purple-black shift (#1A0A1A). The coat does not move correctly in wind. It hangs wrong. As if the body beneath does not have the internal physical displacement that makes clothes move naturally.
**Hood:** Always up. Part of the threat. He is not hiding -- the hood is structural to the wrongness of his appearance.
**No visible accessories.** Nothing that catches light.

---

## The Demon Hounds

The Dog Tamer commands a pack of demon hounds. They are his primary weapon in Episodes 1-3; he uses them as scouts and as pressure before engaging directly.

### Hound Description
**Base:** Corpse-like rotting dogs. Not cartoon zombie dogs -- real animal decomposition aesthetic. Grey-brown decaying flesh (#2A1F1A). The texture is wrong.
**Size:** Larger than any real dog -- shoulder height mid-thigh on an adult human standing. The size is wrong in the way that makes the brain register threat before the eye confirms scale.
**Shadow leaks:** Pure black smoke erupts from joints (shoulder, elbow, knee, hip joints), from wounds in the flesh, from the eye sockets. Not constant -- it pulses slightly, like breathing.
**Eyes:** Two points of dim red light (#5C0000) per hound. Barely visible. Noticeable in darkness.
**Sound:** Near-silence. The most important detail. They do not bark. They do not growl. Their footsteps are too soft for their size. They breathe with a wrong rhythm -- slightly slower than expected, and slightly arrhythmic.

### Hound Behaviour
- Scout first. They appear before the Dog Tamer. They are how he finds blood signatures.
- They do not attack immediately. They surround. They stop and wait. They allow the thing they have found to understand its situation before the attack comes.
- In combat they are fast and silent. The attack is a burst of sudden motion from perfect stillness. No warning sounds.

---

## Voice Direction

None. The Dog Tamer does not speak.

**Sound presence:** Ambient sound drops in high frequency when he is on screen. Not dramatically -- the scene does not announce this. The audience feels it before they identify it. Things that should be present in the ambient sound (distant traffic, wind, environmental hum) are slightly reduced. Sound is avoiding him.

This should be implemented in Audacity/DaVinci by applying a very slight high-frequency cut to the base ambient audio track in any scene he is in.

---

## Function in Each Episode

**Episode 1:** Arrives at the estate. Finds the flat. The mother is there. The hounds are with him. This is a confirmation task -- he has found the signature, now he is confirming the blood. The mother's death is not the mission. She was in the way of the mission. He is not cruel -- he is methodical. He then senses the brothers and the activated power. He does not engage immediately. He does not rush.

**Episodes 2-3:** Presence in the background. The brothers are trying to understand what happened. The Dog Tamer is circling -- he knows the blood has activated, which makes this more interesting than expected. He is not reporting to Malgris yet. He is observing.

**Episode 4:** The direct confrontation. He engages the brothers properly with the remaining hounds. It is a real fight. He is a real general. The brothers win because shadow power is specifically effective against the ancient demonic, and because Gabriel does not stop. The Dog Tamer's defeat is the moment the story escalates.

**Episode 5 (brief):** His defeat registers with Malgris. We see the signal land. He is gone.

---

## Combat Notes

In combat, he uses the hounds as the primary attack vector and controls the space around them. He himself engages only when the hounds are insufficient.

His personal combat is physical but augmented -- the wrong proportion of his body extends when he reaches for something, further than it should. His reach is wrong. Add this to the animation: arm extensions that should not reach do reach.

He takes serious damage before going down. He is a demon general. The brothers need to genuinely earn this win.

---

## Blender Build Notes

- Humanoid rig, extended proportions -- height up, limbs slightly wrong-length
- Coat: long cloth simulation with deliberate weight settings to make it hang wrongly
- Face: keep in shadow via lighting setup -- no specific detailed face modelling required
- Hounds: separate modelled asset. Base is rigged quadruped, deformed. Decomposed flesh texture in Krita. Particle system on joints/wounds for shadow leak smoke.
- Hound scale: roughly 1.5x German Shepherd size
- Tilt: apply slight rotation to the neck bone in the Dog Tamer rig. 5-8 degree persistent tilt. This is baked into the base pose, not a pose variation.
