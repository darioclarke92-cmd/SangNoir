# SANG NOIR — SERIES BIBLE
## Season 1: "Blood Remembers"

---

## Logline

Two French-British brothers from South London discover they are the last of a legendary demon-hunting bloodline — as the ancient warlord who destroyed their clan in medieval France rises again to finish the job in England.

---

## The Hook

This is not a story about chosen heroes. Tre and Gabriel Pourfendeur did not ask for any of this. They are not special by choice — they are special because something tried very hard to make sure they never existed. They are the thing that survived. That is their power and their problem.

---

## Tone

**Primary reference:** Afro Samurai — stylish, brutal, heavy contrast, cinematic weight. Every frame looks intentional. Violence has consequence. Style is never decoration — it is information.

**What this series IS:**
- A South London coming-of-age story wrapped in a demon-hunting mythology
- Grounded in real place, real speech, real grief — with supernatural stakes
- Visually dark, high contrast, cold colour palette broken by moments of pure black violence
- Emotionally restrained — the brothers show pain through action, not words
- A story where the villain is more ancient and considered than the heroes are ready for

**What this series IS NOT:**
- Bright shonen energy — no yelling, no powering up through friendship speeches
- American superhero tone — powers are inherited burden, not gift
- Over-explained — the world has rules but the characters do not narrate them
- Redemption arc for the villain — Malgris does not want forgiveness. He wants them dead.

---

## World Rules

### The Shadow Inheritance
- Power is not learned. It is remembered by the blood.
- Activates only under mortal threat the first time. Can be trained after.
- Visual: Pure black smoke. Near-invisible in darkness. Coils and wraps like smoke. Then detonates on impact like an explosion. Silent in transit — half second — then brutal.
- No colour, no glow. Absence of light made physical.
- The weapons exist in the shadow dimension already, waiting. Tre's great sword. Gabriel's dual daggers. They have always been there.
- Cost: Extended use causes temporary emotional hollowness — numbness, dissociation. Madeleine warns Tre about this. Gabriel ignores her.

### The Mark
- Both brothers carry a Pourfendeur mark — appears when power awakens. Located on the forearm.
- The mark is the channel through which Madeleine communicates.
- It burns cold when demons are near.

### Demons
- Exist in a parallel layer that bleeds into the human world at dark points — tunnels, abandoned places, places of old violence.
- Not random monsters. They have hierarchy, generals, a chain of command that runs up to Malgris.
- They can hold human form for limited periods. The Dog Tamer does not bother. He is too far gone.
- Malgris is at the top. Ancient. Patient. Has been operating for centuries.

### The Shadow Dimension
- The brothers' weapons exist here. Madeleine exists here. Clan history is encoded here.
- Access is through the mark, through near-death, or through Madeleine's invitation.
- Visually: not a parallel world — more like the dark side of the same world. Same locations, drained of colour, filled with floating fragments of clan memory.

## Characters — Overview

### TRE POURFENDEUR (Protagonist)
Age 20. Angry survivor becoming a hunter. Blunt. Commands. Dry anger. Max two sentences unless truly broken. His power is the most controlled of the two — shadow as a weapon he wields, not a force that wields him. Yet.

### GABRIEL POURFENDEUR (Brother / Anti-Hero)
Age 18. Calculated rage. Colder than Tre. More instinctual with power. His arc ends the season as something the demons should fear — but he never becomes the villain. He is the cost of having no guidance. He crosses lines Tre will not.

### MALGRIS (Warlord — Main Antagonist)
The reason everything in this story exists. Ancient. Almost polite. Never raises his voice. Destroyed the Pourfendeur clan in 1300s France. Thought he finished them. He is not panicking that he was wrong. He is simply correcting the error. His respect for the brothers — which he cannot entirely suppress — is the most dangerous thing about him.

### MADELEINE POURFENDEUR (Ancestor Guide)
Founder of the clan. Centuries old. Appears in Tre's mark as a warrior in her prime. Not comforting. Not warm. Tactical, blunt, precise. Speaks only when the blood is ready to hear it. Her relationship with Tre is the emotional spine of the series.

### THE DOG TAMER (First Antagonist, Ep1-4)
Demon general. Humanoid but wrong. Sent to locate clan blood. His defeat is what signals Malgris. He does not speak. He does not need to.

### REMY POURFENDEUR (The Father — deceased)
Knew everything. Said nothing. Died fighting alone to buy his sons years of safety. The revelation of his full story is the emotional centre of Episode 9. He was not absent. He was protection.

## Season Arc Structure

**Episodes 1-4: Awakening**
Brothers go from ordinary to reluctant hunters. The enemy is real but local. Stakes are personal — survival, grief, each other. The world is opening up under them.

**Episodes 5-8: Escalation**
The true scale becomes clear. Malgris is not a monster — he is a strategy. Each champion is a test. Gabriel is passing tests in ways that worry Tre and Madeleine. The brothers are getting stronger but diverging.

**Episodes 9-10: Weight**
The past lands on the present. Remy's story. The France massacre. The brothers understand for the first time what they are the last of — and what that cost. Malgris appears in person and does not fight. He measures them. They are not ready. He knows it.

**Episodes 11-12: Reckoning**
The brothers are as ready as they will ever be. Tower Bridge. All remaining pieces. Malgris in demon form. Madeleine manifests physically for the only time. The season ends with Malgris defeated — but Gabriel has crossed a line in the final fight that cannot be uncrossed. Season ends open.

## Writing Voice — Rules

1. Short sentences. Real South London speech. Not BBC English, not American.
2. Silence used as a tool. A look, a pause, walking away — these replace words.
3. No monologuing. Emotion is read through action.
4. Tre: Blunt. Commands. Dry anger. Max two sentences unless truly broken.
5. Gabriel: Even less. Colder. His silence is louder than Tre's words.
6. Madeleine: Precise. Old. No warmth. Tactical. Speaks in observations, not advice.
7. Malgris: Measured. Almost polite. Never raises his voice. Every word deliberate.
8. The Dog Tamer: Does not speak.
9. Action carries emotion — not dialogue.
10. The London setting is never explained or noted — it simply is. The dialects, the estate architecture, the tube station lighting — it is all background that is never commented on by the characters who live in it.

## Visual Identity

- **Art style:** Afro Samurai — heavy outlines, deep blacks, high contrast, limited colour palette
- **Renderer:** Blender EEVEE with toon shading
- **Colour mood:** Cold. Night. The estate is blue-grey concrete. The Underground is harsh yellow-white. The spiritual realm is desaturated, fragment-filled.
- **Shadow power:** Pure black. No colour. Absence of light. Coils like smoke, detonates like an explosion.
- **Malgris human form:** Colour-perfect. His suit is the only thing fully black in a scene — not shadow-black. Tailored-black. A different kind of absence.

---

## What Success Looks Like

A viewer who has never heard of SANG NOIR watches Episode 1 and by the end is not asking "what is this?" They are asking "what happens next?" The world is legible. The brothers are real. The threat is felt. The grief of the mother's death lands before the powers do. That order matters.

Every subsequent episode should feel like it earned its place. No filler. No padding. At 2-3 minutes per episode, every second is load-bearing.
