# SANG NOIR -- MALGRIS HISTORY
## The Warlord

---

## What Malgris Is

Malgris has always been a demon. There is no human origin, no fall from grace, no deal that went wrong. He was never anything else.

This matters. He does not carry the weight of what he lost. He has no nostalgia, no grief, no version of himself he is trying to recapture. He is entirely and only what he is -- an ancient demonic intelligence with no residual humanity to appeal to.

He is ancient. Not the oldest thing in the demon hierarchy -- there are older forces that do not move through the world the way he does -- but old enough that most of what existed when he first began operating is now gone and forgotten.

He operates with the calm of something that has outlasted everything it has encountered. He is not young enough to be angry. He is not bored. He is pursuing a specific goal with the patience of something that does not experience time as pressure.

---

## Historical Timeline (Approximate)

**Pre-medieval period (approximate, exact era unknown):**
Malgris was already operating before most of recorded demon history. What he was doing, who he fought, how he built his position in the demonic hierarchy -- largely unknown. What is known: he has been at or near the top of demon power structures for long enough that no one currently alive remembers a time before him.

**Medieval period:**
He began inserting himself into human power structures in northern France -- not for territory in the human sense, but because controlling human political systems provided cover, resources, and reach. This is when the Pourfendeur clan first identified him as a target.

**1300s France -- The Pourfendeur Engagement:**
The first bloodline that genuinely slowed him. Not stopped -- slowed. The clan's shadow power is the one thing in the natural world that is specifically effective against his kind of ancient demonic substance. They are literally made of the counter-force. This is why he could not simply walk into their holdings. He needed strategy. He needed numbers. He needed months of preparation.

The fact that it took that much to end a family of 60 people is information. He has not forgotten it.

**Post-France:**
He moved on. Other territories, other campaigns. England was always background -- he had little interest in the island. The Pourfendeur extermination was administrative cleanup, not a priority project.

He was wrong to treat it that way. He knows it now.

**Present -- London:**
The Dog Tamer's detection of Pourfendeur blood in South London is the first serious surprise Malgris has had in a very long time. His response is methodical, not reactive. He does not rush. He sends a general first. He watches what happens.

When the Dog Tamer is defeated by two untrained boys who have had their power for less than a week -- Malgris reassesses.

## Motivation

Legend.

The Pourfendeur clan killed thousands of demons over their operational history. Hundreds of years of active hunting. Generals destroyed. Ancient presences ended. The clan's body count in the demonic hierarchy is significant -- significant enough that within demon culture, the Pourfendeur name carries fear.

Malgris wants to be the one who ended them.

Not for revenge. Not for territory. Not because he is threatened. Because in the hierarchy of demon achievement, destroying the last of a bloodline that has killed thousands of your kind is a permanent claim to legend. An immortal reputation. Something that gets spoken about for as long as demons exist.

He almost earned it in France. He did not. That incompletion is the only thing that approaches irritation for him -- not because he is angry, but because the record is unfinished. An almost is not a legend. Only a clean ending is.

The brothers are the final chapter. Ending them does not just close a loose end -- it writes his name into demonic history permanently. He is not in a hurry. He will do it correctly this time.

His measured, almost polite manner is not a mask for anything. He genuinely is not angry. He respects the brothers' bloodline -- their father hunted well alone for years, without training, without clan support. That legitimately impresses him. And it changes nothing. A worthy opponent makes the legend cleaner, not harder.

When he says "Your father was brave. It didn't help him." -- he is not being cruel. He is telling them the truth. He respected Remy. That respect did not save Remy and it will not save them. That is the information he is delivering.

## Malgris and Respect

This is the most dangerous and complex aspect of his character.

Malgris genuinely respects strength well applied. He respected the French Pourfendeur clan. He respected Remy. He is beginning to respect the brothers, against his preference.

This respect does not change anything. It does not make him hesitate. If anything, it makes him more thorough, because he knows a respected opponent needs to be fully finished, not nearly finished. The France job was nearly finished. He does not make that mistake again.

His respect manifests as attention. When he is dismissive, he does not look at you. When he is watching the brothers, he is giving them the full measure of what he is. That is its own kind of threat.

## Human Form

Tall Black man. Immaculate tailored black suit. Unhurried movement. Ancient intelligence behind composed eyes. Almost polite in speech -- complete sentences, considered word choice, never raises his voice.

His suit is correctly black in a world where "black" means something different. Shadow power black, demon black, night black -- these are all deep and present. Malgris's suit is tailored black. The precise opposite kind. He wears it as a statement about what he is: not shadow, not darkness, not chaos. Something more organised than all of that.

He does not use human form as disguise. He does not need to -- he can move through London without being detected. The human form is simply comfortable for him. It is how he thinks and operates at his most precise.

---

## Demon Form

Revealed in Episode 11 when the brothers have earned his full attention.

Massive. The suit fragments. Underneath is not flesh -- it is compressed shadow armour that assembles as he transforms. Not the same shadow as the Pourfendeur power. Older shadow. Denser. The armour is dark charcoal and black in layered plates, cracked at the joints and across the face with deep dark red light bleeding through. Like something made of stone that has been burning from the inside for centuries.

His face in demon form: Cracked stone surface. Red light bleeding through the cracks. Solid dark red eyes.

He is significantly larger -- not monstrous-large, but imposing-large. The scale of someone who has stopped needing to fit into rooms designed for normal people.

He is slower in demon form. More considered. Every movement has weight. This is not weakness -- it is what absolute physical power looks like when you do not need to rush.

**Key rule:** He only transforms when he respects the opponent. Shifting to demon form is not desperation -- it is acknowledgment. When he transforms in Episode 11, he is telling the brothers: you have earned the real version of me.

## The Champion System

Between the Dog Tamer's defeat and the Tower Bridge confrontation, Malgris dispatches champions. These are high-level demon combatants, each with different capabilities. They are not sent to kill the brothers -- they are sent to test them.

Malgris is building a complete picture of what the brothers can do before he arrives himself. He will not walk into a fight without knowing exactly what he is walking into. He did that in France and it cost him more than expected.

Each champion's defeat tells him something:
- What powers they have unlocked
- How they work together (or don't)
- Where Gabriel's threshold is
- Whether Madeleine is actively guiding them (she is -- partially)

By Episode 9, when he appears in person for the first time without fighting, he knows them well enough to say: not yet. He is not ready for them to be ready yet.

## What Malgris Does Not Know

- That Madeleine is accessible through the mark. He does not know a Pourfendeur ancestor survived in the shadow dimension. He assumed the massacre ended all clan memories.
- That Gabriel is approaching a threshold that makes him something different from a standard Pourfendeur hunter. Malgris tracks the bloodline signatures, not the psychological state of the carriers.
- That there is at least one other Pourfendeur blood survivor elsewhere. (Season 2 thread -- do not introduce in Season 1.)

These are the variables he has not accounted for. They will matter.
