# SANG NOIR -- CLAN HISTORY
## The Pourfendeur Bloodline

---

## Name

**Pourfendeur** -- from Old French. To cleave. To split through. To cut down.

Not a warrior's boast. A description of method. The clan fights with precision, not force. The shadow does not overwhelm -- it finds the exact point and splits it. The name is a philosophy.

## Clan Symbol

A knife with angel wings. Angular, stylised -- not decorative. A weapon engraving. Not designed to be beautiful.

Old French inscription carved into the handle and blade: **"Les morts ne vivront pas"** -- *The dead shall not live.*

This symbol appears on Tré's greatsword and both of Gabriel's daggers. It is the only visual continuity between the medieval clan and the brothers. They do not know what it means when they first see it. Madeleine will tell them.

## Founding Myth

The shadow power was not a gift. It was a deal.

The first Pourfendeur -- a great-great-uncle whose name the clan recorded but rarely spoke -- made a bargain with a minor demon in a moment of catastrophic loss. His village had been destroyed. The Church had called it God's will. He did not accept that answer. He found something in the dark and made an arrangement: power to take revenge, in exchange for something the demon could feed on.

He got the power. He took the revenge. It worked.

What he did not understand was that the feeding arrangement did not end with him. The deal bound to the bloodline. Every Pourfendeur born after carries both the power and the cost -- the emotional hollowness that comes with extended use is not a side effect. It is the demon's payment, still collecting across generations.

The clan discovered this eventually. They did not undo it -- they could not. They learned to manage it. To set limits on power use. To train discipline before the shadow activated so that when it came, the carrier had habits already in place.

Tré will learn this through Madeleine -- carefully, with weight, because the cost is real.

Gabriel will never hear it the right way. He will interpret the hollowness as clarity. As the noise of emotion being stripped away, leaving only the signal. He is wrong. But he will not find out he is wrong until it has gone further than Madeleine intended.

---

The Pourfendeur clan emerged in France in the early medieval period. Not nobility. Not peasantry. Something apart -- a family that stood between the human world and the demon layer that bled into it at the soft points of history.

Their power is not magic in the traditional sense. It is not granted, not learned, not stolen. It is inherited at the blood level -- cellular, encoded. The children of Pourfendeur blood carry the shadow in them from birth. It waits. It only activates when the blood is threatened with death.

This means the clan has always had a delay between birth and becoming -- a window of vulnerability, usually in adolescence or early adulthood, when the shadow wakes. The clan historically protected young members through this window. Remy had no one protecting him when his activated. He faced it alone.

## Medieval Period -- 14th Century France

The clan was at its peak in the 1300s. A network of extended family and trained hunters spread across northern and central France. Not large -- 12-15 active blood members at peak. An elite family, not a force. Each generation trained in combat before the shadow arrived, so when it came, they had something to work with.

They operated in silence. French society in the 1300s was not equipped to hold the knowledge that demons existed -- the Church would have burned the clan as witches faster than the demons would have killed them. The Pourfendeur family maintained surface lives as merchants, minor landowners, craftspeople. The hunting was done at night, by family units, without record.

**What they hunted:**
Not all demons. The clan were not exterminators of every darkness. They hunted specifically the organised demonic presences -- the ones that planned, that accumulated power, that built toward something. A lone minor demon predating on travellers was not their concern. A demon general establishing a domain was.

Malgris was building a domain. He had been for decades, slow and patient, inserting himself into the power structures of the region. By the 1350s, several nobles who held significant territory in northern France were either controlled by Malgris or were Malgris, wearing a human face.

The Pourfendeur clan identified this. And moved against it.

---

## The Confrontation with Malgris

The clan had fought demon generals before. They had not fought anything like Malgris.

He is older than the clan. Older than France as a political entity. He had seen bloodlines rise and fall before. When the Pourfendeurs moved against him, he was not alarmed. He was mildly inconvenienced.

His response was not a counterattack. It was an elimination. He did not meet them in the field -- he removed the clan's social footing first. The merchant contacts were cut. The land holding was seized by a controlled noble. The clan was isolated from the human world that provided their cover. Then, over a period of months, he sent generals to find and kill them.

Not quickly. Methodically. He wanted to ensure no branch survived. No cousins hidden in other regions. No children spirited away.

He almost succeeded.

---

## The Massacre

The final consolidation happened in the late 1350s. A single night. The last gathered members of the clan, who had retreated to a rural holding. Malgris sent the Dog Tamer (then a different incarnation of the same rank) leading a pack of demon hounds and three other demon generals.

The clan fought. They were outnumbered, isolated, and had been running for months. They killed two of the generals. The shadow power performs its best against the ancient -- but exhaustion and numbers overpower even the best bloodline.

By dawn, Malgris believed the Pourfendeur clan was extinct.

**What he did not know:**
A younger branch of the bloodline had settled in England -- specifically London -- a generation before the massacre. They had been there long enough that the French connection was weakening. They were, by the 1350s, a London family with French ancestry who carried something in their blood that had not yet activated.

Malgris's survey of surviving bloodline was incomplete. He felt the France signatures extinguish. He did not detect the English branch. They were too far. Too diluted in the bloodline count. Too dormant.

He closed the chapter and moved on.

## Madeleine Pourfendeur

> NOTE: Whether Madeleine is the clan founder or the last great leader killed in the massacre is a creative decision. Current working assumption: She is the last great leader -- the one who led the clan in the final confrontation with Malgris. The founding myth is separate and earlier.

Madeleine led the clan in its final years. She is the one who identified Malgris as something unprecedented. She strategised the confrontation. She fought the longest in the massacre. She was the last to die.

Her presence persisting in the shadow dimension -- accessible through the mark -- is not supernatural accident. Before she died, she encoded herself into the clan's shadow inheritance. A deliberate act. She knew someone might survive, somewhere. She left herself as a warning and a weapon for whoever came next.

She has waited centuries for blood ready enough to receive her.

She is not a comforting ancestor. She watched her entire clan die. Her patience is not serenity -- it is the controlled anger of someone who has been waiting a very long time to finish something.

## The London Branch -- Path to the Brothers

The London branch lived quietly for centuries. No activation. No hunting. The shadow dormant in successive generations.

The bloodline spread, married into London families, became South London across generations. French names softened. The ancestral knowledge was never passed down -- there was no one left in France to pass it.

**Remy Pourfendeur:**
Born London. South London, Peckham adjacent. The shadow activated in him in his late teens -- nearly killed him because he had no context for what was happening. He survived alone, worked out what it was through a period of terrifying self-discovery. Found fragments of clan history in the shadow dimension, which Madeleine could access through him but chose not to -- she could feel he was not ready and not trained enough for full contact.

Remy trained himself. Hunted alone. Was very good, eventually. Met a woman, had two sons, tried to keep the hunting and the family completely separate. The thinking: if the demons cannot find the family, the family is safe. His death, years before the story starts, was the result of this strategy failing -- a demon general tracked him to his hunting ground and killed him. He did not tell anyone.

The mother knew he hunted. Did not know what. When he died, she told the boys he left.

**Tre and Gabriel:**
Born to Remy and a South London mother with no clan blood. They carry Remy's shadow inheritance but it has been dormant their entire lives. They have no idea what they are.

Until Episode 1.

## Why Malgris Is Back Now

Malgris has never stopped operating. After France, he continued -- other bloodlines, other territories, other projects. England was never a primary focus.

What changed: Modern surveillance. Not human surveillance -- demon intelligence. The Dog Tamer (this generation) detected residual bloodline signature in South London. Faint. Very old. But unmistakable Pourfendeur blood.

Malgris was not alarmed. He was surprised. And then thorough.

He dispatched the Dog Tamer first. Not to fight -- to confirm. To find exactly where the blood is. And then to remove it.

His first line to the brothers, when he finally appears in person: "Your father was brave. It didn't help him."

He says this not to taunt. He says it as information. He wants them to understand the scale of what they are facing. He wants the ending to be clean.
