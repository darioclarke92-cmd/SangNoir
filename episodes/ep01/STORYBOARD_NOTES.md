# SANG NOIR — EPISODE 1
## Storyboard Notes — "The Night the Lights Went Out"

**Purpose:** Panel-by-panel visual direction for Krita storyboard drawing.
Each entry describes what to draw, where the key elements sit in the frame, and what the panel communicates emotionally. Not every shot needs a storyboard panel — only the key beats. Shot reference numbers from SHOT_LIST.md.

**Krita canvas:** 1920×1080px (16:9). Use a 6-panel grid per page (3×2). Rough — clarity over polish. These are for production reference, not presentation.

---

## PANEL NOTES

---

### PANEL 01 — Establishing: The Estate Block
*Shot 01*

**Draw:**
The estate block from across a rain-slicked road. Fourteen storeys. Narrow windows stacked up the face of it. A few lights on — most dark. Orange sodium streetlight in the foreground catches the wet road surface, creating a long reflection that points toward the block like an arrow.

**Composition:**
- Block: centre-right, tall, filling two-thirds of the frame height
- Road in the foreground: bottom quarter, wet, reflective
- Sky: dark, low cloud, no stars
- A bus moving in the background (left edge) — the only movement

**Mood:** Cold. Indifferent. The block does not care who lives in it.

**Colour direction for Krita:**
- Sky: near-black (#0A0A10)
- Sodium light: dull amber (#B87020)
- Road reflection: a stripe of amber across dark grey
- Block face: dark grey-blue (#1E2230), windows as dark rectangles with occasional warm orange squares (lit flats)

---

### PANEL 02 — The Brothers Walking
*Shot 02*

**Draw:**
Tré and Gabriel from the front, slightly below eye level. They walk toward the viewer. Tré is taller, slightly ahead. Gabriel half a step behind, right of frame. Both are relaxed — this is not a tense walk.

**Composition:**
- Both brothers centred, lower half of frame
- Estate blocks and street behind them receding into background
- Streetlight source above and left — half their faces lit, half in hard shadow
- Rain: light — indicated by streaks in the air, wet shine on clothing

**Key detail:**
- Tré's hoodie oversized, hood down, grey
- Gabriel's puffer jacket — matte black, clean-cut silhouette
- Their faces: neutral. This is Tuesday.

**Mood:** Ordinary. Normal. This is what normal looks like before it ends.

### PANEL 03 — Montage Strip (3 panels across one row)
*Shots 06–10 condensed*

**Draw as three small panels in a horizontal strip:**

*Panel 3A — The Door Hand-Off*
- A door open a crack. Two hands in the gap — one passing a small package, one passing folded notes. The rest is dark.
- No faces. Just hands and a sliver of light from inside.

*Panel 3B — The Car Park*
- Wide. A single overhead sodium light in a flat grey car park. A man on a concrete bench, mid-40s, already reaching into his pocket. Tré and Gabriel walking in from the left — small in frame.
- Gabriel is two steps back from Tré. This is his position.

*Panel 3C — Tré's Hands*
- Close on Tré's hands counting and folding notes. Walking motion implied by the slight blur at frame edges.
- Notes are worn — not crisp.

**Mood across all three:** Quiet. Efficient. This is business they know how to do.

### PANEL 04 — First Sight of the Dog Tamer
*Shot 13*

**Draw:**
The walkway viewed from behind the brothers. Their silhouettes fill the bottom of the frame — two dark shapes. The walkway extends away from them in a long corridor of harsh estate lighting. At the far end, partially lit, partially shadow: the Dog Tamer.

**Composition:**
- Brothers: dark silhouettes, bottom-third, centred
- Walkway: perspective lines drawing the eye toward the Dog Tamer
- Dog Tamer: small — far back, top-third, right of centre
- Hounds: low shapes around his feet, low to the ground, barely visible

**Key detail — the Dog Tamer:**
- Too tall for the space he is standing in (his head near the walkway ceiling)
- Head slightly tilted
- Coat perfectly still despite the wind that is visibly moving other things in the frame (a plastic bag, a piece of paper)
- NO face visible yet — just shape and wrong proportions

**Mood:** Wrong. The estate is familiar. He is not. The distance makes it worse, not better.

### PANEL 05 — The Flat Window
*Shot 16*

**Draw:**
Looking up the face of the block from below. Third-floor window. The light inside: warm, ordinary. A silhouette of normal life behind it — a curtain edge, nothing threatening.

The wrongness is context, not image. This flat window is the most normal thing in the episode. That is the point.

**Composition:**
- Shot from below, tilted up — block face fills the frame
- The lit window: top-centre, warm amber against the cold grey block face
- Slight rain in the air — streaks across the frame

**Mood:** The light means someone is home. That meaning is about to change.

---

### PANEL 06 — Running
*Shot 18*

**Draw:**
Tré and Gabriel in full sprint, side-on. Low angle. The estate blocks tower above them. The ground is wet, their feet kicking up water.

**Composition:**
- Both brothers mid-frame, side-on, mid-stride
- Ground: low in frame, wet, reflecting the streetlights
- Block walls: rushing past behind them, blurred at the edges
- The energy: everything forward

**Key posture:**
- Tré ahead, arms pumping, face forward
- Gabriel behind — he is fast. Lighter build, but not losing ground.

**Mood:** Urgency. No panic — focused. These two know how to run.

### PANEL 07 — Katalina on the Floor
*Shot 22*

**Draw:**
The kitchen. Wide shot from the doorway. Katalina on the floor, back against the base of the kitchen units. She is small against the room. Tré in the doorway left. Gabriel coming in right.

**Composition:**
- Kitchen: wide, fluorescent overhead light — harsh
- Katalina: bottom-centre of the frame. The units tower behind her.
- The kitchen is ordinary — dishes, a pot on the stove, a tea towel on the handle. Domestic. She chose this floor to rest on.

**Key detail:** She is looking at her sons. Her face is already shifting to urgency.

**Lighting for Krita:**
- Overhead strip light: harsh white-yellow (#E8E0C0)
- Kitchen units: cream-grey (#C8C0B0)
- Floor: worn linoleum — beige
- Katalina's skin tone: warm brown, the same blood as the brothers

**Mood:** The hardest shot. She is on the floor in her own kitchen. Draw the ordinary-ness of the kitchen hard. The contrast does the work.

---

### PANEL 08 — Katalina's Face (Last Words)
*Shot 24*

**Draw:**
Katalina's face close. Slightly above her eye level — the sons looking down at their mother. She is looking up at both of them. Her eyes are steady. She knows what she needs to say and she is saying it.

**Key expression note:**
She is not crying. She is not broken. She is focused. A South London mother in the last moments telling her sons what matters. Her eyes are full but controlled. The emotion is contained in the steadiness of her gaze, not in tears.

**Composition:**
- Her face: fills the frame, slightly below centre (we are looking down at her gently)
- Overhead light: harsh shadow under her eyes and chin
- Edge of the kitchen unit behind her: grounding her

**Mood:** This is the most important face in the episode. Draw it with care.

### PANEL 09 — The Mark Appearing (Tré)
*Shot 29*

**Draw:**
Tré's left forearm. Inner side. The mark forming. The sleeve has ridden up. In near-darkness — only the faint glow of Gabriel's phone on the floor casting cool light upward.

**The mark visual:**
- The skin is disrupted — not a wound, not colour, but impression
- Angular shapes forming in the skin — think geometric, deliberate, like something that was always there emerging
- Dark brownish-grey against the medium-dark brown of his skin (#4A2E1A mark on #8B5E3C skin)
- Very slightly raised, like a brand settling in

**Lighting:**
- Cool, low, from below — the phone glow
- Everything dim. The mark is the most visible thing in the shot.

**Mood:** A door opening that can never be closed.

### PANEL 10 — The Mark Appearing (Gabriel) — Mirror Panel
*Shot 30*

**Draw:**
Exact mirror of Panel 09. Gabriel's right forearm. Same mark. Same moment. Same light.

**Key instruction:**
Draw this panel as a deliberate mirror of Panel 09. The audience must feel the simultaneity. In the storyboard, place these two panels side by side with no dividing line between them — they bleed into each other.

### PANEL 11 — The Brothers Look at Each Other
*Shot 31*

**Draw:**
Both brothers in close frame. Between them — the camera catches both faces. They are looking at each other. No words. The mutual recognition.

**Key expression:**
- Tré: jaw set. Processing. The anger is present but he does not know what to aim it at yet.
- Gabriel: completely still. He is already past shock. He is already thinking.

**Composition:**
- Tré left, Gabriel right
- Both faces in frame, centre tight
- Dark behind them — the kitchen is dark now

**Mood:** The last moment before their lives formally change. Draw the stillness.

---

### PANEL 12 — Tré's Shadow Wakes
*Shot 32*

**Draw:**
A thread of pure black smoke from Tré's forearm. It rises — but not like steam. It coils. It bends with direction. In the near-dark kitchen, it is the blackest thing in the frame — not grey, not dark — black. An absence.

**Visual spec:**
- Smoke: a single thread initially. Not a cloud. One tendril, coiling, purposeful.
- From the mark on his forearm — starts there, curls upward
- The rest of the frame: dark, near-shapeless. The smoke is the focal point.

**Mood:** Something waking up. Old. Patient. There is no warmth in this smoke. It is not fire.

---

### PANEL 13 — Gabriel's Shadow Presses
*Shot 33*

**Draw:**
Gabriel's fists. The air around them darker than the dark around them. Not smoke — pressure. The shadow does not curl or thread — it pushes outward. The darkness around his knuckles is denser than the darkness of the room.

**Visual contrast with Panel 12:**
- Tré's shadow moves upward, coils, has shape
- Gabriel's shadow pushes outward in all directions, has no single direction
- Same black, different behaviour
- This difference is the character difference made visual

**Mood:** Raw. Uncontained. Something that does not know yet how to be controlled.

---

### PANEL 14 — Dog Tamer Observing
*Shot 35*

**Draw:**
The Dog Tamer's face. Close. In the corridor, at the corridor end light's edge. His face is in the shadow below the light — the light catches the top of his head and the shoulders, his face is in half-dark.

He is looking at the marks on the brothers' arms.

**Key visual:**
- His face: human-shaped but wrong. The proportions are not quite right — the jaw slightly too long, the eye spacing slightly too wide. Nothing you can point to. Just wrong.
- His eyes: dark. Still. Not threatening, not curious — observing. Recording.
- His coat: perfectly still. The corridor has air movement. The coat does not register it.

**Mood:** He has found what he came to find. He is filing it. He will leave and tell something much older what he saw tonight.

---

### PANEL 15 — The Empty Corridor (Final Shot)
*Shot 38*

**Draw:**
The corridor. Empty. Long. The stairwell light at the far end — flickering, about to die.

**Composition:**
- The corridor: perspective lines leading to the far end
- The stairwell light: at the end — the only light source, pulsing
- Nothing else. No one. No hounds. No Dog Tamer. As if none of it happened.
- Rain outside somewhere — a window at corridor end shows the wet black sky

**Mood:** London is still out there. It did not notice. This corridor holds what happened in it and does not show it. The light flickers and that is all.

**Colour direction:**
- Floor: concrete grey, slightly wet (#2A2A2A)
- Walls: estate-block cream darkened with age (#3A3830)
- Stairwell light: flickering amber-white — pulse it with a colour note: (#C0A050 → #000000 → #C0A050)

**This is the last image before CUT TO BLACK. Hold it.**

## DRAWING NOTES FOR KRITA

- **Line weight:** Strong outlines (3–4px brush) on character silhouettes and key shapes. Thinner (1–2px) for interior detail.
- **Colour:** These are storyboard panels — tone them with flat colour fills, no rendering. Mood, not finish.
- **Faces:** Draw clearly enough to read expression. Not finished character art — readable emotion is the goal.
- **Shadow smoke (Panels 12–13):** Use a soft airbrush in pure black (#000000) at ~60% opacity, layered. The smoke should look soft-edged but black — not grey.
- **[SOCIAL] panels:** Mark these with a vertical guide line at x=420 and x=1500 indicating the 9:16 safe zone centre.
