# GABRIEL POURFENDEUR
## Character Production Sheet

---

## Overview

**Role:** Brother / Anti-Hero
**Age:** 18
**Heritage:** Same as Tre -- French-British, born South London, Pourfendeur blood through father.

Gabriel is the younger brother and the more dangerous one. Where Tre fights with controlled anger, Gabriel fights with calculated rage -- emotion fully present but processed into something cold and precise. His mother's death does not break him. It clarifies him. That is more dangerous than grief.

His arc ends Season 1 as someone who has become genuinely frightening. Not the villain. Never the villain. But the season closes with him having crossed a line in the final fight that cannot be uncrossed. Tre knows it. Madeleine warned them both it was coming.

---

## Physical Description

**Build:** Slim. Lean muscle -- not built up, but there. Lighter build than Tre.
**Height:** 5'9"
**Skin:** Same base as Tre -- medium-dark brown, warm undertone. Brothers. The resemblance is visible but not identical.
**Hair:** Cornrows -- flat, tight, close to the head. Clean lines. His are more maintained than Tre's twists.
**Eyes:** Slightly lighter brown than Tre's. The expression default is neutral, watchful. He blinks less than normal.
**No distinguishing marks** (pre-series). His mark appears on the right forearm, mirroring Tre's left -- when it activates.

## The Mark

Right forearm, inner side. Mirror of Tre's. Activates simultaneously in Episode 1.

His mark develops differently -- same clan symbol but slightly more irregular. The shadow that comes through him is less disciplined than Tre's. By late season, the mark spread is further along his arm than Tre's equivalent -- the shadow is bleeding more into him, and he is not stopping it.

---

## Voice Direction

**Speech pattern:** Even less than Tre. Gabriel's silences are the loudest moments in any scene he is in.

**Default register:** Flat. Not cold for performance -- genuinely flat. He has processed the emotion already and is operating beyond it.

**Key rule:** He does not explain himself. He does not justify. He acts. If someone asks him why he did something, he does not answer.

**Examples:**
- (Silence. Tre says "We need to move." Gabriel is already moving.)
- "I heard you." (first time he responds to Madeleine -- three words, then nothing more)
- (After going too far in Ep4 -- Tre says "Stop." Gabriel looks at what he has done. Looks at Tre. Walks away. No words.)

**The exception:** To Tre. Only to Tre does Gabriel say more than minimum. And even then -- brief. But real. These moments are the most important in their relationship.

---

## Power Specification

**Shadow type:** Raw. Uncontained. The same bloodline power as Tré but without the discipline the clan once trained into its hunters. Gabriel has no framework -- the shadow in him erupts because that is what it does when it has no boundaries. He is not directing it. He is channelling it by instinct, and the instinct is violent. Where Tré has learned to hold the power as a tool, Gabriel IS the tool -- the power runs through him rather than from him.

**Combat style with power:**
- Dual daggers -- faster than Tre's sword. Close range. Violent.
- He gets inside the reach of opponents and stays there. No distance combat.
- The shadow on him coils tighter -- it does not reach outward as much as Tre's. It accelerates him, augments impact, appears as a corona of black smoke at his hands and feet during combat.
- Where Tre's shadow is a weapon he holds, Gabriel's shadow is something he IS during a fight.

**The dual daggers:**
- Shorter than Tre's sword. Matched pair. Slightly different lengths -- the right one slightly longer.
- Same matte black as Tré's sword. Clan symbol on the flat of each blade -- knife with angel wings, "Les morts ne vivront pas" carved into the fuller. Same inscription as Tré's sword, applied to both daggers.
- They form from the shadow at his hands faster than Tré's sword forms from the ground. Less ceremony. More violence.

**Going too far:**
From Episode 4 onward, there are moments where Gabriel does not stop. A champion is defeated and he continues. Not frenzy -- it is cold. He applies more force than the situation requires because he has decided that will not be a variable again. He is removing certainty of threat. Madeleine's warning to Tre is partly about this: Gabriel is using the power as elimination, not defence.

**Cost:**
The emotional hollowness side effect is progressing faster in Gabriel because he uses more power, more completely. He does not experience it as hollowness -- he experiences it as clarity. This is the danger. He thinks the cost is beneficial.

## Character Arc -- Season 1

| Phase | Episodes | State |
|---|---|---|
| Before | Pre-Ep1 | Quieter than Tre. Holds himself somewhat apart. Not cold -- just separate. |
| Clarity | Ep1-2 | Mother dead. Gabriel does not go through shock the way Tre does. By Episode 2 he is cold and functional. Tre finds this more frightening than his own grief. |
| Information | Ep3 | The France flashback. Gabriel absorbs it as tactical data. He is building a picture. |
| Threshold | Ep4 | The Dog Tamer fight. He goes too far for the first time. He is not disturbed by this. That is the tell. |
| Divergence | Ep5-8 | Each fight, the line moves. He crosses it each time with less effort. In Episode 8, Tre says stop twice. Gabriel hears him both times. |
| Malgris | Ep9-10 | Malgris appears in person. Gabriel watches him. He is not afraid. He is measuring distances. This is the moment Malgris notices Gabriel is a different kind of problem. |
| The line | Ep11-12 | Tower Bridge. In the final fight, Gabriel does something irreversible. He crosses to a place Tre cannot follow. He wins. He is alive. He is not the same person. |

---

## Key Moments to Nail

1. **Episode 2, morning after.** Tre is barely functioning. Gabriel has made breakfast. Is already dressed. The contrast is the scene.
2. **Episode 4, going too far.** No music. Tre stops. Gabriel just -- keeps going. Then stops himself. Looks at his hands. Not because he regrets it. Because he is checking the mechanism.
3. **Episode 7, the moral choice.** Involves a civilian. Gabriel's choice is not cruel -- it is logical. That is the problem.
4. **Episode 12, after the line.** The fight is over. Malgris is dealt with. Gabriel is standing. He looks at Tre. Tre looks back. Season ends here. No words. The look is the ending.
