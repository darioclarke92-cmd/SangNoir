# SANG NOIR — EPISODE 1
## "The Night the Lights Went Out"

**Duration target:** 2–3 minutes
**Format:** Screenplay (scene-by-scene, production-ready)

---

## CHARACTERS IN THIS EPISODE

- TRÉ POURFENDEUR (20)
- GABRIEL POURFENDEUR (18)
- KATALINA POURFENDEUR (mother, early 40s)
- THE DOG TAMER (demon general, no dialogue)
- DEMON HOUNDS (pack — no dialogue)

---

## COLD OPEN

---

### SCENE 1 — EXT. PECKHAM ESTATE / BACK STREETS — NIGHT

*A wet South London night. Orange sodium streetlight reflects off black tarmac. Low drone of distant traffic. Grime instrumental — sparse, cold, rhythmic — low under the scene.*

*A block of flats. The kind that stacks fourteen floors and forgets the people inside them. Walkways above. Stairwell light flickering.*

*TRÉ and GABRIEL walk together. Not hurrying. The pace of two people who know every pavement on this estate. Tré is taller, grey hoodie, hood down, Air Forces already marked by the rain. Gabriel half a step back, black puffer, cornrows tight, hands loose at his sides.*

*They're not tense. This is ordinary to them.*

---

**TRÉ**
*(not looking back, voice low)*
Devlin still ain't picked up.

**GABRIEL**
He will.

**TRÉ**
Said that yesterday.

**GABRIEL**
*(a beat)*
He will tonight.

*Tré glances at him. Reads something in the certainty. Nods once. They keep walking.*

---

*Quick cuts — MONTAGE, half a minute total. No dialogue. The music does the work.*

*— A door opens. A hand appears. Package changes hands. Money back. Nod. Door closes.*

*— A car park. A man twice their age sits on a bench. He sees them coming and is already reaching for his pocket. Gabriel stands two steps behind Tré. Just watching. That's enough.*

*— A corner. A woman, thirties, takes the package without looking either of them in the eye. Pushes the notes into Tré's hand. Closes her front door quietly. The kind of quiet that means kids asleep inside.*

*— Tré counts the notes as they walk. Folds them. Puts them away.*

*This is normal. This is Tuesday.*

---

**GABRIEL**
*(walking)*
Mum said she needs gas money before Friday.

**TRÉ**
I know.

**GABRIEL**
Said she's short.

**TRÉ**
*(dry)*
She's always short. I heard her.

*Gabriel goes quiet. That's not irritation. That's agreement.*

*They turn back toward the estate.*

---

### SCENE 2 — EXT. ESTATE BLOCKS / MAIN WALKWAY — CONTINUOUS

*They come around the corner of the main block. The entrance ahead. Stairwell light above the door, half dead, pulsing.*

*Tré slows.*

*Gabriel stops.*

*Something is wrong.*

*At the far end of the walkway — partially lit, partially swallowed by shadow — stands a figure. Tall. Too tall. The proportions are not right. Head slightly tilted. Long dark coat that does not move in the wind, even though there is wind. Still in the way only something that does not breathe stands still.*

*Neither brother speaks.*

*Around the figure's feet — darker shapes. Low to the ground. Moving slowly. Dog-shaped. But not dogs. Bigger. Wrong in the body — like something that was once a dog and isn't anymore. Shadow leaks from the gaps in them, from joints, from the places flesh should close.*

*The figure does not move. It is not looking at them.*

*It is looking up.*

*They follow its gaze.*

*The flat. Third floor. The light is on.*

---

**TRÉ**
*(already moving)*
Mum.

---

*They run.*

---

### SCENE 3 — INT. STAIRWELL / THIRD FLOOR CORRIDOR — CONTINUOUS

*They hit the stairwell door, take the stairs three at a time. The lights up here are fully dead. Gabriel pulls out his phone torch without being told. They reach the third floor corridor.*

*The flat door is open.*

*Not broken down. Not smashed. Just — open. Like someone knocked and it swung wide.*

*They exchange a look. Move toward it.*

---

### SCENE 4 — INT. KATALINA'S FLAT — CONTINUOUS

*The hallway is dark. The kitchen light is on at the end of it. The flat is small, warm, familiar — photos on the wall, a coat rack with too many coats, the smell of food still on the air.*

*Tré goes through the hallway first. Gabriel behind him.*

*The kitchen.*

*KATALINA is on the floor, back against the kitchen units. She is alive. Barely. Something has happened — not a wound that reads clearly, but something that has taken something out of her. Like she fought something that was not a person and the fight cost her.*

*She looks up when they come in. Her face changes. Relief. Then something else — urgency.*

---

**GABRIEL**
*(down beside her immediately)*
Mum. Mum, look at me.

**KATALINA**
*(voice low, focused — not panicking)*
I'm here. I'm here. Stop —

*Tré is already scanning the flat. Looking for whatever did this. The flat is empty. Only them.*

**TRÉ**
*(to Gabriel, clipped)*
Nothing in here.

*He crouches down beside his mother. Takes her hand. Her grip is still strong. That matters to him.*

**KATALINA**
*(to both of them — she needs them to hear this)*
Listen to me. Don't go—
*(breath)*
Don't go looking for trouble with this. Promise me.

**TRÉ**
Don't talk. We need to—

**KATALINA**
Tré.

*He stops. She has used that particular weight in his name that only mothers have.*

**KATALINA (CONT'D)**
Look after each other.
*(to Gabriel, then back to Tré)*
Whatever happens. Whatever you find out.
*(a beat — this is everything)*
Don't lose each other.

*Neither of them speaks. Tré's jaw is set. Gabriel is completely still. The silence is its own answer.*

*Her hand loosens in Tré's.*

*The kitchen light flickers once.*

*Goes out.*

---

### SCENE 5 — INT. KATALINA'S FLAT — MOMENTS LATER

*Dark. The only light is Gabriel's phone, face down on the floor, giving off a faint glow against the linoleum.*

*Tré is on his feet. He is standing in the middle of the kitchen. Not moving. Not looking at anything in particular.*

*Gabriel is still on the floor.*

*Silence.*

*Then —*

*Tré's left forearm burns. Not heat — cold. A pressure. Under the skin, along the inner forearm. Like something drawing a shape.*

*Gabriel's right forearm. The same.*

*At exactly the same moment both of them look down at their arms.*

*On Tré's skin — a mark forming. Dark brownish-grey on the skin. Angular. Deliberate. A shape neither of them has seen before but that does not feel unknown.*

*Gabriel's right forearm. Mirror of it.*

*The same mark. At the same time.*

*They look at each other.*

---

*The flat door is still open.*

*From the corridor outside — the distant sound of something low and wrong. Not a dog's footstep. The sound of something that breathes at the wrong intervals. Getting closer.*

---

*Both brothers stand.*

*The marks on their arms pulse once — cold, definitive.*

*From Tré's forearm: the first thread of pure black smoke curls into the air. Not rising like steam. Coiling. Moving with direction.*

*From Gabriel's hands: not smoke — pressure. The air around his fists darkens. The shadow does not coil. It presses outward. It wants to expand.*

*The same power. Two different bodies. Two different natures.*

*Neither of them understands what this is.*

*Neither of them steps back.*

---

### SCENE 6 — EXT. ESTATE CORRIDOR — CONTINUOUS

*The Dog Tamer stands at the end of the corridor. Same stillness as before. Head slightly tilted. The hounds are arranged behind him — low, motionless, shadow bleeding from their joints.*

*He looks at the brothers.*

*He looks at the marks on their arms.*

*At the smoke coiling from Tré. At the darkness pressing from Gabriel's hands.*

*He does not attack.*

*He observes.*

*A long beat — no music, no ambient. Just the distant sound of London at night and the wrong breathing of eight demon hounds.*

*Then the Dog Tamer turns.*

*Walks away.*

*The hounds follow.*

*They vanish into the dark at the end of the corridor as if they stepped through it.*

---

### SCENE 7 — INT. KATALINA'S FLAT / KITCHEN — MOMENTS LATER

*The corridor is empty. The marks are still present on both forearms but the smoke has gone. The darkness around Gabriel's hands is gone.*

*The brothers stand in the doorway of the flat.*

*They do not look at the corridor. They look back into the flat. At the kitchen. At what the kitchen holds.*

*Tré is the one who moves first.*

*He walks back inside.*

*Gabriel follows.*

*The door stays open behind them.*

---

*FINAL FRAME: The corridor. Empty. Wet. The stairwell light flickering at the far end.*

*Silence.*

*CUT TO BLACK.*

---

*No music. No title card.*

*End of Episode 1.*

---

## VOICE NOTES

- **No voiceover** at any point. Zero exposition delivered by narration.
- Katalina's final words are calm — not hysterical. She is a South London mother. She handles crisis by focusing. The urgency is in her eyes, not her voice.
- Tré's line "Mum." before he runs — one word. That is the whole emotional register.
- The Dog Tamer scene: **complete silence from both brothers.** No gasping, no "what is that." They are South London. Their first response to something threatening is not noise.
- The mark activation moment: silent. No dialogue. The scene ends on the mark and the smoke. Let it land.

## PRODUCTION NOTES

- **Episode length target:** 2 min 30 sec — 3 min max. Every scene is tight.
- **No exposition dumps.** The audience does not know what the marks are. The brothers do not know. This is correct.
- The Dog Tamer does not attack because he has what he came for: confirmation that Pourfendeur blood is alive and has activated. His job is reconnaissance. He reports to Malgris. Episode 2 opens the morning after.
- **Katalina:** briefly visible, does not need long screen time. The weight of her death comes from what she says and the brothers' reactions — not from extended death scene.
- **First use of shadow power:** Intentionally incomplete. The smoke curls. The darkness presses. Neither brother does anything with it. This is not a power reveal — it is a power *appearance*. The reveal comes later.
