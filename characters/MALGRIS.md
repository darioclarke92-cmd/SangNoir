# MALGRIS
## Character Production Sheet

---

## Overview

**Role:** Main Antagonist / Warlord
**Age:** Ancient. Pre-medieval. Exact era TBD (see lore/MALGRIS_HISTORY.md)
**Classification:** Demon warlord -- formerly human, transformed through centuries of accumulation

Malgris is the reason everything in this story exists. He is not the adversary the brothers chose -- he is the adversary that chose them, centuries before their birth, by failing to fully eliminate their bloodline in 1300s France.

He is not a monster. He is organised, patient, and ancient. He respects things that earn his respect. He does not respect the brothers yet. He will. That is the trajectory.

---

## Human Form

### Physical Description
**Build:** Tall. Not exceptionally broad -- the height does the work. Well-proportioned, the kind of physical presence that owns a room without seeming to try.
**Height:** 6'4"
**Skin:** Deep brown. Immaculate. No visible marks, scars, or imperfections. He has not accumulated physical damage the way a human does.
**Face:** Strong structure. Not young-seeming, not old-seeming -- ageless in a way that registers as strange only if you look carefully.
**Eyes:** Dark brown in human form. No supernatural quality -- this is deliberate. He controls what he reveals.
**Hair:** Close-cropped. Neat. No styling flourishes.

### Clothing
**Suit:** Tailored. True black (#000000). Not shadow-black, not night-black -- the precise black of expensive cloth, perfectly cut. This is the only clean, tailored black in any scene. Everything else in the darkness around him is textured, imperfect black. His suit is sharp.
**Shirt:** White. Crisp. The contrast is deliberate.
**Pocket square:** Deep red (#8B0000). His only colour. Used in every scene. Barely visible. But it is always there.
**No tie.** The top button of the shirt is fastened. Nothing about his appearance is careless.

### Movement
Unhurried. Every movement is the exact movement required. He does not fidget, gesture unnecessarily, or shift weight. When he is still, he is completely still. When he moves, he moves with complete purpose.

This stillness is the first thing that reads as wrong. Humans move slightly all the time -- micro-adjustments, shifts, nervous habits. Malgris does not.

## Demon Form

First full reveal: Episode 11 (Tower Bridge), when the brothers have earned it.

### Physical Description
**Scale:** Significantly larger than human form. Not a monster in scale -- inhabits the space differently. Where his human form owns a room, his demon form makes a room feel smaller.
**Height:** Approximately 7'6" -- enough to be visually imposing but not cartoon-large.

### The Transformation
The suit fragments -- it does not tear, it separates cleanly, like the precision that held it together is withdrawing. Underneath, compressed shadow armour assembles. Not a costume change. An emergence. The armour was always underneath.

### Armour Description
- **Colour:** Layered dark charcoal and blacks (#0A0A0A, #1A1A1A, #2A2A2A)
- **Texture:** Dense, compressed. Like shadow that has been compacted under pressure over centuries. Not smooth -- it has layers, like geological strata.
- **Cracks:** At every joint (shoulders, elbows, knees) and across the face/chest -- deep red-orange light bleeds through (#5C0000). Like something burning behind stone.
- **Purpose of the light:** His age. The things he has absorbed over centuries bleeding through the compression.

### Face in Demon Form
- Stone-grey cracked surface
- Red-orange light bleeding through cracks
- Eyes: Solid dark red (#8B0000). No pupil. No iris. Just the red.
- The face is not monstrous in shape -- it is recognisably Malgris. That is what makes it worse.

## Voice Direction

**Register:** Measured. Considered. Almost polite. He speaks in complete sentences. He does not abbreviate.
**Volume:** Never raised. If anything, he speaks more quietly in high-stakes moments. He is not trying to intimidate with volume -- the content does that.
**Pace:** Slightly slower than normal speech. Each word is chosen. There is space between sentences.
**Tone:** Respectful in a way that is worse than contempt. He treats the brothers as worthy of being spoken to truthfully.

### Key Lines (from Series Bible)
**First line to the brothers (Episode 9):** "Your father was brave. It didn't help him."
- Said without cruelty. He is delivering information.

**Additional voice direction notes:**
- He does not use slang. Not because he is stiff -- because language precision matters to something this old.
- He never asks questions he does not already know the answer to.
- When he compliments, he means it. That is more unsettling than insults.

## Power Specification

**Human form:** He operates through control and presence. He can affect demonic forces in his vicinity -- direct, amplify, redirect. He does not use physical power in human form. He does not need to.

**The champion relay:** Malgris in human form sends and receives through the champions. He can see through them to a limited degree. When the Dog Tamer is defeated, Malgris feels it.

**Demon form -- combat:**
- Movement: Heavy. Every strike is final-force. He does not escalate -- he begins at maximum.
- His shadow material is older and denser than the Pourfendeur shadow. The counterforce relationship is real -- the brothers' shadow can genuinely hurt him. This is why he is thorough.
- He can extend the compressed shadow from the armour into environmental attacks -- walls of pressure, compression fields. Not flashy. Oppressive.
- He does not use speed. He does not need to.

## Key Scenes and Behaviour

**Episode 5 -- Receiving the signal:**
Malgris is alone. The Dog Tamer's defeat registers. He is still for a moment. Then he moves.to a new position and begins arrangements. No expression of alarm. No words. The leitmotif plays once. Then silence.

**Episode 7 -- Walking Canary Wharf:**
He is assessing London. Also forcing a moral choice on the brothers through a civilian situation -- not because he needs to, but because he wants to observe how they make decisions. He does not intervene in the fight. He watches from a distance.

**Episode 9 -- First in-person appearance:**
He finds the brothers. Does not attack. Does not threaten explicitly. Speaks to them. Tells them the truth about their father. Tells them what they are. Leaves. This is his most precise form of cruelty -- giving them the full picture so they understand what they are walking into. He wants them to know and come anyway, because if they come anyway there is nothing to correct for later.

**Episode 10 -- Showing them France:**
He would not do this if he thought they had any chance. He shows them the massacre -- through the shadow dimension, projected somehow (exact mechanism TBD during scripting) -- because he believes that seeing the full scale of what he did to their entire clan in a single night will extinguish whatever resolve they are building. He is wrong. He does not know this yet.

**Episodes 11-12 -- Tower Bridge:**
He transforms when Tre and Gabriel are still standing after everything he has sent. Not for drama. Because he acknowledges they have earned it.
