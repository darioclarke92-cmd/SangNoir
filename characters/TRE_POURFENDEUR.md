# TRE POURFENDEUR
## Character Production Sheet

---

## Overview

**Role:** Protagonist
**Age:** 20
**Heritage:** French-British. Born South London. The Pourfendeur bloodline runs through his father.

Tre is not a hero in the classical sense. He is an angry young man trying to hold his life together, who discovers that his blood has been carrying a war for centuries without his knowledge. His arc is from angry survivor to deliberate hunter -- someone who fights not because he wants to, but because he is the only thing standing between the people he still has and the thing that keeps coming.

---

## Physical Description

**Build:** Medium. Not lean, not bulky. The build of someone who moves a lot -- estate upbringing, physically active without being gym-trained. Functional muscle.
**Height:** 5'11"
**Skin:** Medium-dark brown. Warm undertone. Reference: deep brown, not ashy, not light.
**Hair:** Short twists. Not long, not elaborate. Low-maintenance. The kind of style that goes a bit longer without care -- by Episode 4 the roots are showing.
**Eyes:** Dark brown. Intense at rest. The default expression is alert, guarded.
**Scar:** Above the left eyebrow. A clean scar from a street fight several years before the story. Not fresh. Healed fully but visible. He does not hide it or reference it.

## Clothing -- Combat State

Same clothing as standard. He does not change into anything. The powers erupt through ordinary clothes. This is deliberate -- the contrast between council-estate teenage clothing and what he can do is part of the visual language.

The hoodie gets torn. He does not replace it with something cooler. He wears a mended version of the same grey hoodie through most of the season.

---

## The Mark

Located on the left forearm, inner side. Appears at the moment the shadow first activates (end of Episode 1). Initially it looks like a burn -- a geometric impression in the skin. Over subsequent episodes, as the power develops, the mark becomes more defined -- a Pourfendeur clan symbol carved into the skin at the cellular level.

It burns cold when demons are near. Not painfully -- a warning signal, like a nerve awakening.

Madeleine communicates through it. Heat and cold and pressure, before actual visions. By Episode 3, actual visions.

**In VRoid/Blender:** Add as a forearm texture overlay -- dark brownish-grey on the skin, slightly raised appearance (achieved through normal map adjustment).

## Voice Direction

**Speech pattern:** Blunt. Short. South London. Not performing anything. When he talks, it matters.

**Default register:** Dry anger. Not explosive -- contained. He is used to being angry and has learned to work through it rather than at it.

**Key rule:** Maximum two sentences unless he is truly broken. In moments of genuine grief or fracture, he talks more than he wants to -- and then stops himself.

**Examples:**
- "Go home." (to a civilian in the way of a fight)
- "That's not my problem." (to Gabriel about a line crossed)
- "Mum's gone." (first line after the mother's death -- to Gabriel, in the flat, not looking at him)

**What he does not do:** Explain himself. Monologue. Ask for help.

---

## Power Specification

**Shadow type:** Controlled. He learns to direct it deliberately. His is the version of the power that is a weapon he holds, not a force he releases.

**Combat style with power:**
- Shadow coils from the mark and the hands
- He directs tendrils as grappling tools before impact
- The great sword emerges from shadow when he reaches for it -- specifically from a dark pool forming near his feet or his shadow
- His shadow use is economical -- he does not use more than the situation needs

**The great sword:**
- Size: Full greatsword. Long, wide at the base, tapers toward the point.
- Material: Matte black -- not shiny. Slightly lighter than the shadow it comes from.
- Carved with the clan symbol along the flat of the blade: knife with angel wings, with "Les morts ne vivront pas" running the length of the fuller
- It does not glow. It is just a sword made of clan-forged metal and shadow.
- He does not know how to fight with it at first. He swings hard and learns. By Episode 6, he has found his form.

**Cost:**
Extended power use causes emotional hollowness. Tre experiences this from Episode 6 onward -- short windows after big fights where he cannot feel things properly. He hides this from Gabriel. Madeleine sees it.

## Character Arc -- Season 1

| Phase | Episodes | State |
|---|---|---|
| Before | Pre-Ep1 | Low-level criminal necessity. Holds the family together instinctively. |
| Shock | Ep1-2 | Mother dead. Powers activated. No framework for any of it. The anger finally has a target but no direction. |
| History | Ep3 | The France flashback lands on him differently than Gabriel. He feels the weight of what they are last of. |
| Hunter | Ep4-6 | He is learning. The sword is real. He starts to fight with purpose rather than reaction. |
| Fracture | Ep7-8 | Gabriel is changing in ways that worry him. He is also changing -- the hollowness side effect. The brothers are pulling in different directions. |
| Weight | Ep9-10 | Father's truth. The scale of what Malgris is. Malgris looks at him and waits. Tre understands he is not ready. He trains for the first time with intent rather than necessity. |
| Reckoning | Ep11-12 | Tower Bridge. Back to back with Gabriel -- the brother he may be losing. Madeleine manifests. He fights the version of Malgris that acknowledges him. Whether he is ready does not matter. He does it anyway. |

---

## Key Moments to Nail

1. **The silence after the mother dies.** Tre does not scream. He stands. Then he walks to Gabriel. That walk is the character.
2. **First sword emergence (Ep6).** He does not react to it with wonder. He just grips it. Of course it is there.
3. **Episode 9 with Gabriel at the grave.** The most he talks all season. It goes badly. He stops mid-sentence and walks away.
4. **Seeing Madeleine in full (Ep10).** She does not comfort him. He did not expect her to. He nods. That's the relationship.
