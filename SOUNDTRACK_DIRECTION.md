# SANG NOIR -- SOUNDTRACK DIRECTION

---

## Core Principle

The music in SANG NOIR works against expectation. South London street scenes do not get orchestral scores. Demon generals do not get loud villain music. The most dangerous moments are the quietest. The scoring creates unease by working beneath the image, not over it.

Three music layers throughout the series:
1. Street/character layer -- UK Drill and Grime. Cold. Rhythmic. Real.
2. Combat layer -- Trap 808s under taiko drums. Modern anime hybrid.
3. Demon/ancient layer -- Deep orchestral strings, almost silent, then sudden.

These layers can coexist, blend, or cut entirely.

---

## Scene Type to Sound Signature

### Peckham/Brixton Estate -- Daytime, Ordinary Life
- Genre: Ambient Grime / UK Garage instrumental
- Tempo: Slow, dragging, purposeful
- Key elements: Sparse hi-hats, low synth tone underneath, occasional bass hit
- Volume: Low. Background. The street sounds carry more weight than the music.
- Purpose: Grounds the world in South London before anything supernatural

### Peckham/Brixton Estate -- Night, Pre-supernatural
- Genre: UK Drill instrumental -- cold, minimal
- Tempo: Slower than standard Drill. Less aggressive, more oppressive.
- Key elements: 808 bass barely moving, sparse snare, space between hits
- Volume: Low-mid. Present but not driving.
- Example Suno prompt direction: "UK Drill instrumental, slow BPM, cold, minimal, South London night, no lyrics, oppressive atmosphere"

### Pre-fight tension (any location)
- Genre: Transition -- Drill bass slowing down, strings emerging underneath
- Tempo: Dropping. Stretched.
- Key elements: 808 slowing, single deep string note held, building sub-bass
- Duration: 10-20 seconds maximum before contact
- DO NOT: Build to a crescendo before the fight. The music should drop, not rise. The fight arrives in near-silence.

### Combat -- Standard fight (brothers vs champions)
- Genre: Trap 808s under taiko drums -- modern anime hybrid
- Tempo: Fast but controlled -- 140-160 BPM trap rhythm with taiko on the heavy beats
- Key elements: Trap hi-hats, 808 bass, taiko on downbeats, occasional orchestral string stabs
- Volume: High. Present. But not drowning SFX -- the shadow power sound must cut through.
- Example Suno prompt direction: "Anime combat music, trap 808 bass, taiko drums, fast BPM, dark, no lyrics, modern anime battle"
- Note: The music cuts or drops to minimal when the brothers use shadow power at full force. The hit lands in near-silence.

### Combat -- Gabriel going too far (Ep4, Ep8, Ep12)
- Genre: Same combat base but degraded -- taiko drops out, 808 becomes irregular
- Tempo: Unstable
- Key elements: The music becomes wrong. Off-beat. Like watching something that should have stopped continue.
- Purpose: The music tells the audience Gabriel has crossed a line before anyone in the scene says it.

### Dog Tamer appearance (any)
- Genre: Almost nothing. Deep orchestral. Single low string note held.
- Tempo: No tempo. Not rhythmic.
- Key elements: Sub-bass drone, barely audible breathing texture, no percussion
- Volume: Low. Creeping.
- The demon hounds: No music. Just their sound design. Silence and footsteps.

### Malgris (human form) -- any scene
- Leitmotif: Single piano note held too long, then a deep bass drop that fades.
  - The piano note: F2 or E2. Held for 4-6 seconds. No reverb.
  - The bass drop: Not aggressive. Low. Slow. Then gone.
  - Total duration: 8-10 seconds. Then silence or ambient street sound resumes.
- Volume: Mid. Present. Impossible to miss but not loud.
- Key rule: Malgris is NEVER scored with villain-sounding music. No dark choir, no aggressive strings. The piano note is ancient and considered. It is not threatening. It simply is.
- First use: Episode 5, when Malgris receives the signal of Dog Tamer's defeat. He does not react visibly. The leitmotif plays once and ends.

### Malgris (demon form) -- Episodes 11-12
- The leitmotif expands: Piano note as before, but the bass drop is larger, slower, and the orchestral strings build slowly underneath.
- When he is fully transformed and fighting: Orchestral only -- no trap, no drums. Ancient. Heavy.
- This is the only fight in the series scored like a classical encounter. Because Malgris is the oldest thing in the story.

### Madeleine's appearances
- Sound: Haunting French strings -- sparse, old, minor key
- Tempo: Rubato (free time, not locked to a beat)
- Key elements: String melody that feels like it came from a different century. Because it did.
- Volume: Low-mid. Intimate.
- The contrast: When Madeleine's string music appears over a South London street scene, it is the most arresting moment in the episode. Two centuries colliding.
- Example Suno prompt direction: "Haunting French strings, minor key, sparse, medieval influence, slow rubato, no drums, ancient and cold"

### Shadow dimension / spiritual realm (Ep3, Ep10)
- Genre: No genre. Experimental ambient.
- Key elements: Reversed string textures, distant echo, very low rumble, occasional fragment of Madeleine's theme surfacing then going under
- Volume: Low. The silence in this space should feel heavier than normal silence.
- Ep10 specifically (Malgris shows them France): The France massacre scene uses the Madeleine string theme played in a lower key, slower, almost broken. As if the memory itself is damaged.

### Emotional scenes -- brothers (grief, confrontation)
- Genre: Nothing, or a single sustained string note at the edge of hearing
- Principle: Do not score the emotion. Let the performance carry it. Music enters only if the scene needs a final push, and then only for the last 10-15 seconds.
- Episode 9 (father's full truth): Madeleine's string theme, very quiet, begins in the final 20 seconds of the scene. Fade in from nothing.

### End of episode -- cliffhangers
- Standard: Music cuts on the final frame. Silence into black. The title card appears in silence.
- Major cliffhangers (Ep1, Ep5, Ep8, Ep12): Single note hit on the cut frame -- piano or low brass. One hit. Then title card in silence.

## SFX Direction -- Key Events

*(Full detail in production/SOUND_DESIGN.md -- this section covers musical/SFX boundary)*

### Shadow power activation
- No musical cue. SFX only.
- Sound: Near-silence. Small air displacement. Like the air moved out of the way.
- Then: Nothing until detonation.

### Shadow detonation impact
- Low, bass-heavy concussive hit. Not a crack or a boom -- a THUD that you feel.
- No musical accent on the hit. The music either stops just before or was already minimal.
- Reverb: Short. Tight. Not echoing.

### Sword emergence (Tre)
- Deep resonant scrape as shadow mass assembles into metal
- Slight low hum on the final assembled state -- like something tuned to a frequency
- No musical cue accompanies this. The sword sound IS the moment.

### Dagger emergence (Gabriel)
- Faster than Tre's sword. More violent.
- Sharp hiss of shadow coiling, then two fast clicks of metal forming
- Slightly higher pitch than Tre's sword -- the daggers are smaller, faster

### Demon hounds
- No soundtrack. SFX only.
- Movement: Wrong footstep sound -- too soft for their size. Like padding with something slightly wet.
- Breathing: Slow, wrong rhythm
- Attack: Complete silence until contact, then impact

### Dog Tamer
- He does not speak. No vocal SFX.
- Presence only: The ambient sound of any location drops in high frequency when he is on screen. Not dramaically -- just slightly. Like sound is avoiding him.

## Suno Workflow Notes

*(For when music production phase begins)*

For each cue type, generate 3-5 variations in Suno using the prompt directions above. Keep the best two per scene type. Label exports as:
- `combat_standard_v1.mp3`
- `malgris_leitmotif_v1.mp3`
- `madeleine_strings_v1.mp3`
etc.

Store all in `assets/music/`. DaVinci Resolve will then use these as a library.

For the Malgris leitmotif: Do not use Suno for this one. Create it in Audacity.
- Single piano note recording (if available) or use a free piano VST
- Hold for 5 seconds
- Fade in a low bass tone under it for 3 seconds
- Both fade out together over 3 seconds
- Export as WAV. This is precise enough that AI generation will not be reliable.
