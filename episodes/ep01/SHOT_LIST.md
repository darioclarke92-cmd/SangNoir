## SCENE 1 — EXT. PECKHAM ESTATE / BACK STREETS — NIGHT

---

**SHOT 01**
- **Type:** WS
- **Description:** Establishing. The estate block from across the road. Fourteen floors. Wet tarmac in foreground reflecting orange sodium light. Rain catching the light. A bus passes in background — London is alive, indifferent.
- **Camera:** Static. Low angle, slightly tilted up toward the block.
- **Lighting:** Orange sodium streetlight dominant. Cold blue-grey in the shadows.
- **Notes:** This is the world. Set it hard. No warmth in this light.

---

**SHOT 02**
- **Type:** MS — tracking
- **Description:** Tré and Gabriel walking toward camera, then past it. Tré in front, grey hoodie, hood down. Gabriel half a step behind, black puffer. Neither looking around. The walk of two people who own this pavement.
- **Camera:** Tracking — starts in front of them, they walk past, camera holds as they continue away.
- **Lighting:** Streetlight from above and left. Their faces partially lit, partially in shadow.
- **[SOCIAL]** — Centre the brothers in the frame throughout.

---

**SHOT 03**
- **Type:** ECU
- **Description:** Tré's Air Forces on wet pavement. Slight splash with each step. The shoes are worn — not fresh.
- **Camera:** Low, ground level. Static.
- **Notes:** Quick cut. Half a second. Sets the physical world before the supernatural enters it.

---

**SHOT 04**
- **Type:** MS — OTS (Tré's shoulder)
- **Description:** Tré speaks over his shoulder. "Devlin still ain't picked up." Gabriel behind him, not looking up.
- **Camera:** Behind and slightly above Tré's right shoulder. Gabriel in mid-ground.
- **Lighting:** Streetlight from left.

---

**SHOT 05**
- **Type:** CU
- **Description:** Gabriel's face. "He will." Flat. Eyes forward.
- **Camera:** Front-facing, slight low angle. Gabriel looking past camera, not at it.
- **[SOCIAL]**

### MONTAGE — HAND-OFFS (Shots 06–10)

*Montage: quick cuts, no dialogue. Approximately 25–30 seconds total across all 5 shots.*

---

**SHOT 06**
- **Type:** ECU
- **Description:** A door opens. A hand emerges. Package changes hands. Money back. Two hands — one giving, one taking.
- **Camera:** Close on the exchange. Door frame in background.
- **Notes:** Do not show full faces. Only hands, partial faces in shadow. Keeps it anonymous, real.

---

**SHOT 07**
- **Type:** WS
- **Description:** Car park at night. Sodium light overhead. A man on a bench sees the brothers approaching and is already reaching into his pocket before they stop.
- **Camera:** Wide, static. Brothers walk in from the left. Gabriel two steps behind Tré as always.
- **Lighting:** Single overhead light, hard shadows below.

---

**SHOT 08**
- **Type:** MS
- **Description:** Gabriel standing. Not doing anything. Just present. Two steps back. That is enough.
- **Camera:** Side angle on Gabriel. Static.
- **Notes:** This shot is about what Gabriel does not do. The stillness is the performance.

---

**SHOT 09**
- **Type:** ECU
- **Description:** A woman's hands pushing folded notes into Tré's palm. Her door starts to close.
- **Camera:** Close on the hands. Door edge in frame.
- **Notes:** The quiet of a door closing meaning kids inside. You don't need to show the kids.

---

**SHOT 10**
- **Type:** ECU
- **Description:** Tré's hands counting and folding notes as he walks. Puts them away.
- **Camera:** Looking down at his hands from slightly above. Walking motion — slight camera shake to sell the movement.
- **[SOCIAL]**

---

**SHOT 11**
- **Type:** MS — two-shot
- **Description:** Both brothers walking. Gabriel: "Mum said she needs gas money before Friday." Tré: "I know." They keep walking.
- **Camera:** Side-on tracking shot. Estate blocks in background.
- **Lighting:** Cold. Blue-grey ambient between the orange streetlights.
- **Notes:** The mundane domestic detail — gas money — is important. These are not just criminals. They are sons.

## SCENE 2 — EXT. ESTATE BLOCKS / MAIN WALKWAY — NIGHT

---

**SHOT 12**
- **Type:** MS — two-shot
- **Description:** The brothers come around a corner and stop. Tré slows first. Gabriel stops behind him.
- **Camera:** Front-facing. Static. They stop in the foreground, the walkway opening behind them.
- **Notes:** The transition from normal to wrong happens in this shot. Their body language shifts. No dialogue needed.

---

**SHOT 13**
- **Type:** WS — deep focus
- **Description:** The walkway. The Dog Tamer at the far end. Small in frame. Partially lit. Head slightly tilted. Long coat still despite the wind.
- **Camera:** Wide. Shot from behind the brothers — their silhouettes in foreground, the Dog Tamer far background.
- **Lighting:** The Dog Tamer is at the edge of a light pool — half lit, half in shadow. This is intentional.
- **[SOCIAL]** — Composition: brothers bottom third, Dog Tamer top third.
- **Notes:** First appearance. The distance is the threat. Do not push in yet. Let the wrong-ness read across the distance.

---

**SHOT 14**
- **Type:** ECU
- **Description:** The demon hounds. Low to the ground around the Dog Tamer's feet. Shadow leaking from their joints. One moves slowly — wrong gait.
- **Camera:** Close on one hound's legs and the shadow bleeding from its knee joints.
- **Notes:** Do not show the full hound yet. Fragment. A leg. A shoulder. The leaking shadow. Let the audience's brain fill the rest.

---

**SHOT 15**
- **Type:** CU
- **Description:** Tré's face. Reading the Dog Tamer. Eyes moving — from the figure, to the hounds, to the flat window above.
- **Camera:** Front-facing tight close-up. His eyes do the work.
- **[SOCIAL]**

---

**SHOT 16**
- **Type:** ECU
- **Description:** The flat window. Third floor. Light on.
- **Camera:** Tilted up — looking up the face of the block to the lit window.
- **Notes:** One second. Then cut to Tré's mouth: "Mum."

---

**SHOT 17**
- **Type:** ECU
- **Description:** Tré's mouth. One word: "Mum." Already moving as he says it.
- **Camera:** Extreme close on the lower half of his face.

---

**SHOT 18**
- **Type:** WS — tracking
- **Description:** Both brothers running. Full sprint. Camera tracks alongside them at pace. The estate blurs at the edges.
- **Camera:** Side tracking. Low angle — ground visible, building height above.
- **[SOCIAL]**

## SCENE 3 — INT. STAIRWELL / THIRD FLOOR CORRIDOR

---

**SHOT 19**
- **Type:** MS — tracking
- **Description:** Tré hits the stairwell door hard and is on the stairs. Gabriel right behind. Three steps at a time. Gabriel's phone torch flicks on without breaking stride.
- **Camera:** Below them on the stairs, shooting up. They come over the camera.
- **Lighting:** Dead overhead lights. Only Gabriel's phone torch — harsh, directional, moving.
- **Notes:** The phone torch creates moving harsh shadows up the stairwell walls. Use this.

---

**SHOT 20**
- **Type:** MS
- **Description:** The open flat door. They reach the third floor and see it: door ajar. Not smashed. Just open.
- **Camera:** End of corridor, looking toward the door. The brothers stop in the foreground.
- **Lighting:** Phone torch from behind them hits the door. The flat beyond is dark except for the kitchen light at the end of the hallway.
- **Notes:** Beat here. A half second of stillness before they move. That pause is everything.

## SCENE 4 — INT. KATALINA'S FLAT

---

**SHOT 21**
- **Type:** MS — tracking
- **Description:** Down the hallway of the flat. Tré first, Gabriel behind. Photos on the wall passing. Kitchen light ahead.
- **Camera:** Tracking — behind them, moving forward slowly.
- **Lighting:** Kitchen light pulls them forward. The hallway around them is dark.

---

**SHOT 22**
- **Type:** WS
- **Description:** The kitchen. Katalina on the floor against the units. Tré in the doorway. Gabriel coming in behind.
- **Camera:** Wide. From the hallway end of the kitchen, looking in. Katalina is small in the frame against the base of the units.
- **Lighting:** Kitchen overhead light — harsh, fluorescent. Cold on everything.
- **Notes:** The wide shot sells how small she looks. The kitchen is ordinary. That contrast matters.

---

**SHOT 23**
- **Type:** CU
- **Description:** Gabriel dropping to his knee beside Katalina. "Mum. Mum, look at me." His voice is not panicked — it is urgent, controlled.
- **Camera:** Side on. Gabriel going down, Katalina in background against the units.
- **[SOCIAL]**

---

**SHOT 24**
- **Type:** CU — Katalina
- **Description:** Katalina's face. She looks up. Relief. Then urgency. Her eyes go to both of them. "Listen to me."
- **Camera:** Slightly above her eye level — looking down at her, but gently, not dominating. Her face fills the frame.
- **Lighting:** Overhead kitchen light. Hard shadows under her eyes.
- **Notes:** This is the most important face in the episode. Hold on her. Do not cut away during her last words.

---

**SHOT 25**
- **Type:** ECU
- **Description:** Tré's hand taking Katalina's. Her grip is strong. His thumb over her knuckles.
- **Camera:** Close on the joined hands. Kitchen floor in background.
- **Notes:** "Her grip is still strong. That matters to him." This shot IS that line.

---

**SHOT 26**
- **Type:** CU — two-shot: Tré and Gabriel
- **Description:** Both brothers in frame. Katalina speaks her last words — "Don't lose each other." They do not speak. Tré's jaw is set. Gabriel is completely still.
- **Camera:** Slightly below, looking up at both their faces. Katalina's shoulder visible bottom of frame.
- **[SOCIAL]**
- **Notes:** Hold this shot through the silence. Let the audience sit in it. Do not rush to the next cut.

---

**SHOT 27**
- **Type:** ECU
- **Description:** The kitchen light. Flickers. Goes out.
- **Camera:** Looking up at the strip light.
- **Notes:** One second of flicker, then black. The darkness in this shot is total for one frame before the next scene.

## SCENE 5 — INT. KATALINA'S FLAT — THE MARK

---

**SHOT 28**
- **Type:** WS — dark
- **Description:** The kitchen. Near-dark. Only Gabriel's phone face-down on the linoleum, casting faint light upward. Tré standing. Gabriel on the floor. Silence.
- **Camera:** Wide. From the doorway. Both brothers visible as silhouettes against the faint phone light.
- **Lighting:** Near-total dark. The phone glow is the only source.
- **Notes:** Let this land. Do not cut immediately. Three seconds of this before anything changes.

---

**SHOT 29**
- **Type:** ECU
- **Description:** Tré's left forearm. Inner side. The mark forming — dark brownish-grey impression moving into the skin. Like a burn backwards.
- **Camera:** Extreme close on the forearm. His sleeve has ridden up.
- **Lighting:** Phone torch glow from below — dim, cool.
- **[SOCIAL]**
- **Notes:** Slow this shot down slightly — not slow motion, but a held beat. The mark appearing is the turn of the episode.

---

**SHOT 30**
- **Type:** ECU
- **Description:** Gabriel's right forearm. The same mark. Appearing at the same time.
- **Camera:** Mirror of Shot 29. Same framing, opposite arm.
- **Notes:** Cut between 29 and 30 twice — Tré's arm, Gabriel's arm, back to Tré's arm — before pulling back to the two-shot.

---

**SHOT 31**
- **Type:** CU — two-shot
- **Description:** Both brothers looking at each other. No words. The mutual recognition: the same thing happened to both of us at the same moment.
- **Camera:** Between them, getting both faces. Eye level.
- **[SOCIAL]**

---

**SHOT 32**
- **Type:** ECU
- **Description:** The first shadow. From Tré's forearm: a thread of pure black smoke curls into the air. Not steam — deliberate. Coiling.
- **Camera:** Close on his forearm, the smoke rising into dark air.
- **Notes:** The smoke is slow here. It is not a burst. It is the first breath of something waking up.

---

**SHOT 33**
- **Type:** ECU
- **Description:** Gabriel's hands. The air around his fists darkens. Not smoke — pressure. The shadow presses outward from his knuckles.
- **Camera:** Close on both fists. The darkening air around them.
- **Notes:** Different from Tré's — it does not coil. It pushes. Even at this first moment, the difference in their power is visible.

---

## SCENE 6 — EXT. ESTATE CORRIDOR — CONFRONTATION

---

**SHOT 34**
- **Type:** WS
- **Description:** The Dog Tamer in the corridor doorway. Same stillness. The hounds behind him. The brothers in the foreground, facing him. The corridor between them.
- **Camera:** Behind the brothers — their backs, the Dog Tamer ahead. The corridor is the space.
- **Lighting:** Stairwell light at the far end. Dog Tamer caught in its edge.
- **[SOCIAL]** — Vertical composition: brothers bottom, Dog Tamer top.

---

**SHOT 35**
- **Type:** ECU
- **Description:** The Dog Tamer's head. Slightly tilted. Looking at the marks on the brothers' arms. Then — slowly — up to their faces.
- **Camera:** Close on his face. The face is in shadow below the light. Eye level.
- **Notes:** He does not speak. He observes. This shot is five seconds minimum. The stillness is the threat.

---

**SHOT 36**
- **Type:** MS
- **Description:** The Dog Tamer turns. No drama. No signal. He simply turns and walks. The hounds follow without sound.
- **Camera:** Front-on to the Dog Tamer and hounds. We watch them walk away.
- **Notes:** They do not run. They do not vanish dramatically — they walk into the dark at the end of the corridor and the dark takes them. The light at the corridor end goes out as they pass it. Natural. Or not.

---

## SCENE 7 — INT. KATALINA'S FLAT — ENDING

---

**SHOT 37**
- **Type:** MS — two-shot
- **Description:** The brothers in the doorway of the flat. Not looking at the corridor. Looking back inside. At the kitchen.
- **Camera:** From inside the flat, looking out at them in the doorway. The corridor is dark behind them.
- **Notes:** They choose to look back in, not out. That is the ending of the episode in one shot.

---

**SHOT 38 — FINAL**
- **Type:** WS
- **Description:** The corridor. Empty. Wet floor. The stairwell light at the far end flickering. Nothing and no one. London still outside somewhere.
- **Camera:** Static. End of the corridor, looking down its length toward the stairwell light.
- **Lighting:** The single stairwell light — pulsing, dying.
- **Notes:** Hold. Ten seconds minimum. No music. No ambient. The corridor breathes.
- **CUT TO BLACK.**

## CAMERA DIRECTION SUMMARY

- **Default lens:** Slightly longer focal length (50–85mm equivalent) — compresses the estate, makes it feel denser
- **Handheld vs locked:** Scene 1 montage = slight handheld. Everything in the flat = locked, static. The world moves; grief is still.
- **Low angles:** Used on the estate to make the blocks feel bigger. Not used in the flat — eye level keeps it intimate.
- **The Dog Tamer:** Always shot from the brothers' perspective — we see him how they see him. Never from his POV.
