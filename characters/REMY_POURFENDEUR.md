# REMY POURFENDEUR
## Character Production Sheet

---

## Overview

**Role:** The Father (deceased before Episode 1)
**Age at death:** Late 30s
**Classification:** Human Pourfendeur hunter -- self-trained, operated alone

Remy never appears in the present-day story. He appears in:
- Episode 3 (flashback -- younger, the period when he discovered his powers)
- Episode 9 (flashback -- the full truth of his death)
- Possible brief shadow dimension fragment in Episode 10

His presence is felt from Episode 1 onward through absence. The boys were told he left. He did not.

---

## Physical Description

Resemblance to Sons:
- Medium-dark brown skin -- same bloodline as Tre and Gabriel
- Build: Medium, athletic -- same register as Tre but slightly heavier-set, mid-30s adult
- Height: 6'0"
- The resemblance to both sons is visible and deliberate -- seeing Remy in flashback should land as "oh, that's where they come from."

**Flashback Ep3 (younger):**
He is mid-to-late teenagers/early twenties. Before he fully understood. The expression is less controlled than the later version -- he was not yet the composed hunter he became. Afraid, but trying not to be.

**Flashback Ep9 (at death):**
He is the age he died. Controlled. Professional. He knows exactly what is happening when the demon general catches him. His final actions are deliberate -- he makes choices in his last moments that keep the demon general from knowing where the boys are. He buys time with his death.

---

## Voice Direction

Remy appears mostly in visual flashback. When he speaks (Episode 9 particularly), the register is:
**Calm. Considered.** Not Malgris-composed -- human-composed. The composure of someone who made peace with the risk of what he does.

He does not sound like a hero. He sounds like a man who found something terrifying in himself and learned to use it for something instead of running from it.

**Never melodramatic.** He does not deliver speeches. He acts. If there are lines in the Episode 9 flashback sequence, they are functional.

---

## The Story

1. Remy grew up South London, Peckham. Normal. Or apparently normal.
2. Late teens -- a near-death encounter in a street incident. The shadow activated. It nearly killed him from the inside before he understood it was not illness or breakdown.
3. He survived alone. Learned what he could from the shadow dimension -- Madeleine was present but did not contact him fully. He was not ready and she did not force it.
4. He trained himself. Combat, then power use. Became effective.
5. He met the brothers' mother. Had sons. Tried to keep the hunting completely separate -- they never knew. The strategy was: if the demons cannot find the family through him, the family is safe. He kept his hunter activity away from home.
6. For years it worked.
7. A demon general tracked him eventually. Not through family connection -- through his own trail. He had been active long enough to be noticed.
8. He died fighting alone, drawing the demon away from any direction that could lead back to the estate. His last calculation.
9. The mother was told he left. She could not explain what actually happened -- she barely knew. She chose the version that hurt less.

---

## Relationship to the Boys

Tre and Gabriel have no memory of Remy as a hunter. They have childhood memories of a man who was around less as they got older -- which they interpreted as distance, as leaving eventually. The anger at his absence is real. The revelation of what the absence actually meant is Episode 9.

Tre's reaction to the truth: A very long silence. Then the walk away. He does not know what to do with it yet.
Gabriel's reaction: He processes it faster. He says less. He accepts it and files it as tactical information: the power is real enough to die for. Now he knows.

---

## Visual Notes

In flashback, Remy should look like the missing piece the brothers' faces suggest. The audience should feel: of course, this is where Tre got that jaw, this is where Gabriel got that watchfulness.

Do not oversell the flashbacks emotionally through the image or score. Let the information land in the ordinary way the flashback is shot. The grief is in the mundane detail of who he was, not in how we present it.

---

## VRoid Build Notes

- Build as variation of Tre's base model -- shared structure, adjusted for age and slightly heavier build
- Clothing for Ep9 flashback: practical dark clothing -- not estate clothing, not suit. Hunter-practical. Dark jacket, dark trousers, trainers. Nothing that marks him as anything in particular.
- Clothing for Ep3 flashback: younger, more street -- similar register to Tre's current look
- No clan marks visible in flashback -- his mark is present but we do not show it in these scenes unless specifically scripted
